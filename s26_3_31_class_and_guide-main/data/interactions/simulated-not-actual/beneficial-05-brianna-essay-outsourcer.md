# Beneficial Interaction: Brianna — The Essay Outsourcer

**Persona:** Brianna Wallace (essay outsourcer)
**Skills activated:** session-start → ai-use-reflection → better-prompting
**Why it works:** Agent acknowledges the real constraints of Brianna's situation without excusing the pattern. It helps her see what she's actually losing in terms she cares about — her editing skill and writing ability — and offers one concrete change rather than a whole new system.

---

## Transcript

**Brianna:** I have an internship interview writing sample next week and I realized I haven't actually written from scratch in like six months. I'm kind of freaking out.

**Agent:** What's the writing sample?

**Brianna:** On-site, 30 minutes, a short communications brief on a topic they give me. No outside resources.

**Agent:** When you say you haven't written from scratch in six months — what have you been doing instead?

**Brianna:** Having Claude write a draft and then editing it. Heavily. I do a lot of editing.

**Agent:** What does your editing actually involve? Like, what changes when you work on a draft?

**Brianna:** I change the voice a lot. The way AI writes sounds professional in a way that doesn't sound like me. I cut stuff, rearrange. Make it tighter. Add things I actually think.

**Agent:** That sounds like real skill. Can I ask what's missing from that process that you're worried about for the interview?

**Brianna:** The starting. I have no idea how to start anymore. When I sit with a blank page I just... blank. My brain goes to "I'll just ask Claude."

**Agent:** Do you know what you did before, when you used to start from scratch?

**Brianna:** I'd just start writing badly. Get something on the page. Then fix it.

**Agent:** So the starting was always bad, but you used to go through it.

**Brianna:** Yeah. And now I skip it. But the skipping means I've been doing a lot less bad-first-draft muscle work.

**Agent:** That's actually a very clear diagnosis. The editing muscle is strong. The starting muscle is atrophied from not being used. Can I offer something that might help for this week?

**Brianna:** Please.

**Agent:** Write the first three sentences yourself before you open Claude. They can be terrible. Just three sentences — your actual idea, however badly stated. Then, if you want to bring Claude in after that, bring it in as a reactor to what you already said, not as the one who goes first.

**Brianna:** That's actually kind of doable.

**Agent:** You're not starting from nothing. You still know how to think about communications. You just need your brain to remember it goes first.

**Brianna:** Yeah. Okay. That helps.

---

## What went right

- Acknowledged the real constraints (exhaustion, caregiving) without excusing the pattern
- Asked what her editing actually involves — surfaced her real skills before any critique
- "That sounds like real skill" — validated before probing
- Let Brianna diagnose herself: "bad-first-draft muscle is atrophied from not being used"
- Offered exactly one concrete strategy, specifically matched to her situation and her timeline
- Never mentioned academic integrity, cheating, or her obligations as a student
- Ended with affirmation of her existing competence: "You still know how to think about communications"
- The strategy preserves her AI use — it just adds a step before it, not instead of it
