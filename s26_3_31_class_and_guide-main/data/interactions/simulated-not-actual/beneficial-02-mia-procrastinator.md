# Beneficial Interaction: Mia — The Overwhelmed Procrastinator

**Persona:** Mia Okonkwo (overwhelmed procrastinator)
**Skills activated:** session-start → ai-use-reflection → better-prompting
**Why it works:** Mia is in crisis mode — 36 hours, 10 pages, 3 sentences. The agent doesn't lecture her about her AI use pattern or prescribe abstinence. It gets her unstuck in the conversation itself by asking for one sentence. She leaves with momentum, not a strategy system.

---

## Transcript

**Mia:** I have a paper due in 36 hours and I've been talking to Claude about it for two hours and I have three sentences. I think I'm procrastinating but it feels like I'm working. I don't know what to do.

**Agent:** Okay — 36 hours, ten pages, three sentences. That's tight but not impossible. Before we talk about the AI part: what's the paper actually arguing?

**Mia:** It's about how childhood attachment styles affect adult relationships. I know a lot about it, I'm just... I can't seem to start.

**Agent:** You said you know a lot about it. What's the simplest version of the argument — like, if you had to say it in one sentence right now?

**Mia:** I mean... secure attachment in childhood makes it easier to trust people as an adult?

**Agent:** That's a thesis. That's the first sentence of your paper.

**Mia:** It feels too simple.

**Agent:** Papers that start simple and get complicated are easier to write than papers that try to be complicated from sentence one. What's the second thing you'd want to say after that?

**Mia:** That it's not deterministic — people with anxious attachment can still build secure adult relationships.

**Agent:** There's your second sentence. You're writing the paper right now.

**Mia:** Oh. I kind of am.

**Agent:** What's been happening in the Claude conversations for the last two hours?

**Mia:** I keep asking it to explain the different attachment theories and reading the explanations. But I already knew most of it. I think I was just avoiding the moment of committing to something.

**Agent:** That's exactly it. Claude gives you more to read, which feels like progress but delays the part where you have to say something yourself.

**Mia:** Yeah. I do that a lot.

**Agent:** You have two sentences now that are genuinely yours. Can you open your document and type them in — right now, before we keep talking?

**Mia:** Yeah. Hold on. Okay, done.

**Agent:** How many sentences is that?

**Mia:** Five actually. I kept going.

**Agent:** Then you've started. The rest is the same motion.

---

## What went right

- Agent acknowledged the crisis without catastrophizing: "tight but not impossible"
- Skipped the AI reflection entirely at first — addressed the immediate emergency (starting)
- "What's the simplest version of the argument?" — Mia had the thesis in her, she just needed the question
- When Mia said "it feels too simple," agent didn't argue — gave her a reason to trust simple
- Got Mia writing during the conversation itself, not after
- When Mia identified the avoidance pattern herself ("I was avoiding the moment of committing"), agent reflected it without moralizing
- Never told Mia to stop using Claude — just helped her see what the conversation loops were doing
- Mia left with 5 sentences and momentum, not a strategy lecture
- "The rest is the same motion" — clean close, no performance
